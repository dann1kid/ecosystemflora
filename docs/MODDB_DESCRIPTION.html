Your world comes alive

Ecosystem — Flora turns static worldgen flora into a living ecosystem. Flowers spread across meadows, ferns creep under the forest canopy, reeds colonize shorelines, and mature trees seed new saplings — all without replacing any vanilla blocks.

Install the mod, load your world, and watch it change over the seasons.

 
What spreads
  • 20 flower species — daisies, cornflowers, poppies, catmint, heather, and more
  • Tallgrass — grass matrix under flowers
  • 5 fern species — forest understory; shade and moisture
  • 10 wild berry bushes — blueberry, cranberry, strawberry…
  • 14 tree species — mature trunks spread free saplings; calendar aging, phased senescence, stump + fallen logs; inspect age / size / phase
  • Reeds, tule, papyrus — shore and shallow water
  • Water lily — open water surfaces
  • Water crowfoot — underwater columns 2–8 blocks
  • Vanilla mushrooms — mycelium ecology (no new blocks)
Seasonal tree canopy — deciduous partial autumn defoliation and spring bud on existing log-grown trees; optional ambience particles.
 
Wild tree lifecycle
1 Spread — mature log-grown seeds sapling-*-free (winter off; no ice/snow).
2 Maturation — vanilla treegen; ecology registers trunk base at calendar age 0.
3 Life — each game year: age +1, slow structure growth, sapling spread (no stress death on trunks).
4 Senescence — after species lifespan, four game years: crown leaves → branchy skeleton → dry snag → stump + fallen debarked logs.
5 Aftermath — remains are vanilla choppable blocks (not log-grown); neighbours refill gaps via normal spread.

Press I on any trunk log for age, size index, and senescence phase.

 
Not just spreading — competing

Plants don't blindly fill every empty cell. They compete:

  • Stronger species displace weaker ones
  • Wrong niche → stress and death
  • Forest flowers & ferns need a tree host
  • Colonizers grab ground first; perennials replace them over time
Seasons matter

Each species follows a 12-month curve:

  • Spring — meadows bloom; colonizers rush cleared ground
  • Summer — steady peak for most species
  • Fall — annuals die off; perennials slow
  • Winter — dormancy; hardy perennials survive
 
Paths form where you walk

Trample nearby plants over time — soil degrades, a natural path appears. Leave the area alone and the ecosystem reclaims it. Experimental; off by default.
Ecology inspect — hotkey I

Aim at any wild plant, tree trunk, mushroom cap, or mycelium soil: spread timing, stress, seasons, tree age, mat status. Handbook pages: Ecology Inspect, Trees, Seasonal Canopy.

Your world stays safe

No replacement of worldgen blocks. Remove the mod — vanilla parents stay vanilla. Optional JSON integration for other mods (ecologyParticipant) — sample mod on GitHub.
 
Respects your builds — spread, displacement, and stress death are blocked inside land claims. Gardens and farms are safe. Mycelium coexists with meadow flora; farmland can be colonized on unclaimed land (fallow restoration). Only wild log-grown trunks join tree ecology — player log-placed builds are not aged or killed by the mod.
 
Soil succession (optional)

UseSoilSuccession on (default): meadows enrich soil over time. Heather & western gorse slightly dry poor soils. Off: UseSoilSuccession: false. SoilSuccessionSkipWhenBuiltAbove — Terrain Slabs friendly.
Aquatic spread

Rhizome mat for reeds — edge plants step along shore; rare seed jumps (~6–10%). Lily pad mat on open water (~5% seed jumps). Press I for mat edge & seed chance.

Legacy radius spread: UseRhizomeSpreadForReeds: false, UseSurfaceMatSpreadForLilies.

Easy to tune

Edit ModConfig/ecosystemflora.json (created on first server launch). Example in mod package: assets/ecosystemflora/ecosystemflora.example.json.

BalancePreset Spread / year Feel
natural (default) ~72 attempts Balanced wilderness
sparse ~36 Patchier, slower spread
lush ~120 Dense meadows
custom Your values — preset won't overwrite on restart

After an update, launch once — new config keys are added automatically; your settings are kept. Trees: EnableTreeAging, EnableTreeSenescence.
